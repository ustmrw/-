Book Bans In 2023? What's next? Book burnings?

June 8, 2023
Opinion Politics

Summary:

Book bans are a futile and foolish attempt to censor ideas and knowledge in the information era, violating freedom of expression and access to information, and wasting resources that could be better utilized for promoting literacy and critical thinking.

Book bans are a futile and foolish attempt to censor ideas and knowledge in the information era. They are not only a violation of the freedom of expression and the right to access information, but also a waste of time and resources that could be better spent on promoting literacy and critical thinking.

Book bans are futile because they cannot stop people from accessing the banned books through other means, such as online platforms, libraries, or bookstores in other jurisdictions. In fact, book bans often have the opposite effect of increasing the curiosity and demand for the banned books, as people want to know what is so controversial or dangerous about them.

Book bans are foolish because they assume that readers are passive and uncritical consumers of information, who will blindly accept and follow whatever they read in a book. This is a patronizing and insulting view of readers, who have the ability and responsibility to evaluate, question, and challenge the ideas and perspectives presented in any book. Book bans also ignore the fact that books can offer valuable opportunities for exploreing, dialogue, and empathy, by exposing readers to diverse and complex views of the world.

Book bans are a relic of the past that have no place in the information era. They are an ineffective and harmful way of dealing with controversial or sensitive topics. Instead of banning books, we should encourage people to read widely and critically, to engage in respectful and constructive discussions, and to form their own opinions based on evidence and reason.
